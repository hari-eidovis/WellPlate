## 1. AI-Powered Features (Leveraging Your On-Device Foundation Models Edge)

### 1.1 AI Meal Photo Recognition
- **What:** Snap a photo of your plate → on-device AI identifies foods, estimates portions, and logs nutrition automatically.
- **Why it stands out:** Most competitors require text input or barcode. Photo-based logging with on-device AI (no cloud round-trip, full privacy) is a killer differentiator.
- **Priority:** P0 | **Importance:** I0

### 1.2 AI Weekly Wellness Report
- **What:** Every Sunday, generate a comprehensive AI narrative summarizing the week — sleep patterns, nutrition trends, stress correlations, exercise consistency, and 3 personalized action items for the next week.
- **Why it stands out:** Competitors show charts; WellPlate *interprets* them. Users get a "wellness coach in their pocket."
- **Priority:** P0 | **Importance:** I0

### 1.3 Predictive Stress Forecasting
- **What:** Using the 30-day stress history + sleep/exercise/diet patterns, predict tomorrow's likely stress level and suggest preemptive actions ("You tend to have high stress on Mondays after poor Sunday sleep — try sleeping 30 min earlier tonight").
- **Why it stands out:** No mainstream app does predictive wellness. Moving from reactive to proactive is a paradigm shift.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 1.4 Conversational AI Health Coach
- **What:** A chat interface where users can ask questions like "Why was my stress high this week?", "What should I eat before my workout?", "How can I improve my sleep?" — powered by Foundation Models with full context of their health data.
- **Why it stands out:** Transforms the app from a tracker into an interactive health advisor. No competitor offers this with *your own data* as context.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I0

### 1.5 Smart Meal Suggestions
- **What:** Based on what the user has eaten today, their remaining macro budget, time of day, and eating patterns — suggest specific meals/foods to hit their goals.
- **Why it stands out:** Goes beyond tracking into actionable guidance. "You need 40g protein and 300 cal for dinner — here are 3 ideas."
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

## 2. Engagement & Gamification

### 2.1 Achievement Badges & Milestones
- **What:** Unlock badges for milestones — 7-day streak, 10K steps 5 days in a row, hitting macro goals 3 days straight, first voice log, first barcode scan, etc. Display in profile as a badge wall.
- **Why it stands out:** Proven retention mechanism. Your streak system is a foundation — badges multiply its motivational power.
- **Priority:** P0 | **Importance:** I1

### 2.2 Daily Wellness Score (Unified)
- **What:** A single 0-100 "WellPlate Score" combining sleep quality, nutrition adherence, activity, stress, and hydration into one number. Show trend over time.
- **Why it stands out:** Users are overwhelmed by 15+ metrics. One unified score gives them a daily "grade" and a clear goal. Apple has Activity Rings — WellPlate has the WellPlate Score.
- **Priority:** P0 | **Importance:** I0

### 2.3 Weekly Challenges
- **What:** Auto-generated personal challenges based on user's weak areas — "Drink 8 cups of water 5/7 days this week", "Log all 3 meals for 4 consecutive days", "Walk 8K steps daily this week."
- **Why it stands out:** Personalized challenges (not generic) based on actual data gaps. Drives behavior change, not just tracking.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 2.4 Habit Streaks Dashboard
- **What:** Expand the current streak system to track multiple habits simultaneously — water streak, meal logging streak, sleep goal streak, exercise streak. Visual grid showing all streaks at a glance.
- **Why it stands out:** Multi-habit streak tracking creates multiple "don't break the chain" motivators.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

## 3. Social & Community

### 3.1 Accountability Partners
- **What:** Pair with a friend/partner. Share daily WellPlate Score, streaks, and challenge progress. Send encouragement nudges. No full social network — just 1-3 close accountability partners.
- **Why it stands out:** Intimate accountability > noisy social feeds. Research shows accountability partners improve adherence by 65%.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 3.2 Family Wellness Dashboard
- **What:** Family members can share their wellness data on a shared dashboard. Parents can see kids' screen time and activity. Partners can coordinate meal planning.
- **Why it stands out:** No competitor focuses on family-unit wellness. Unique positioning.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

### 3.3 Anonymous Community Benchmarks
- **What:** "Users like you (same age, goals, activity level) average 7.2 hours of sleep. You're at 6.5." Privacy-preserving comparisons without social profiles.
- **Why it stands out:** Social proof without social pressure. Motivating without exposing personal data.
- **Priority:** P3 | **Importance:** I3

## 4. Notifications & Smart Reminders

### 4.1 Contextual Smart Reminders
- **What:** Not dumb time-based reminders. Smart triggers:
  - "You haven't logged lunch and it's 2 PM" (based on meal gap)
  - "Your stress tends to spike around now — try a 5-min breathing exercise" (based on patterns)
  - "You're 2 cups short on water today" (based on hydration tracking)
  - "Great job! You hit your step goal early today" (celebration)
- **Why it stands out:** Context-aware notifications that feel helpful, not nagging.
- **Priority:** P0 | **Importance:** I0

### 4.2 Morning Briefing Notification
- **What:** A single morning notification: "Good morning! You slept 7.2h (good). Yesterday's WellPlate Score: 78. Today's focus: hit your protein goal (you've been short 3 days in a row)."
- **Why it stands out:** Proactive, personalized, and actionable — not "Don't forget to log!"
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 4.3 Stress Spike Alert
- **What:** When real-time heart rate data indicates elevated stress (sustained high HR + low HRV), send a gentle notification with a breathing exercise or suggestion.
- **Why it stands out:** Real-time health intervention. Moves from daily summaries to in-the-moment support.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

## 5. Advanced Health Insights

### 5.1 Correlation Engine
- **What:** Automatically discover correlations in user data: "When you sleep > 7.5h, your stress score is 23% lower the next day", "Coffee after 3 PM correlates with 40 min less sleep", "Your best exercise days follow nights with > 1.5h deep sleep."
- **Why it stands out:** No competitor surfaces personalized correlations. This is the "aha moment" feature that makes users tell friends about the app.
- **Priority:** P0 | **Importance:** I0

### 5.2 Menstrual Cycle Integration
- **What:** Track or sync menstrual cycle data (HealthKit provides this). Show how cycle phases correlate with energy, stress, cravings, sleep quality, and exercise performance.
- **Why it stands out:** Most health apps ignore cycle-based wellness variations. Huge underserved market (50% of users).
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 5.3 Body Composition Tracking
- **What:** Log or sync body weight, body fat %, muscle mass over time. Show trends alongside nutrition and exercise data.
- **Why it stands out:** Connects the dots between input (food, exercise) and output (body composition). Currently you collect weight in onboarding but don't track changes.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 5.4 Supplement & Medication Tracking
- **What:** Log daily supplements and medications. Track adherence streaks. Cross-reference with vitals (e.g., "Your blood pressure has trended down since you started magnesium").
- **Why it stands out:** Adjacent health tracking that no pure nutrition app does well.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

### 5.5 Symptom Journal
- **What:** Log symptoms (headache, bloating, fatigue, brain fog, joint pain, etc.) and correlate with diet, sleep, and stress. AI identifies potential triggers.
- **Why it stands out:** Turns WellPlate into a diagnostic tool. "You report bloating 80% of the time after eating dairy" is life-changing insight.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

## 6. Enhanced Food & Nutrition

### 6.1 Meal Planning & Recipes
- **What:** AI-generated weekly meal plans based on goals, dietary restrictions, and eating history. Include simple recipes with nutrition pre-calculated.
- **Why it stands out:** Bridges the gap between "what to eat" and "what did I eat." Most trackers are retrospective; meal planning is prospective.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 6.2 Restaurant Menu Scanner
- **What:** Point camera at a restaurant menu → AI identifies items and shows estimated nutrition. Tap to log.
- **Why it stands out:** Solves the #1 pain point of nutrition tracking: eating out. No competitor does this well.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I1

### 6.3 Grocery List Generator
- **What:** Based on the week's meal plan or nutritional gaps, generate a smart grocery list grouped by store section.
- **Why it stands out:** End-to-end nutrition management from planning to shopping to logging.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

### 6.4 Fasting Timer
- **What:** Built-in intermittent fasting tracker with common protocols (16:8, 18:6, OMAD, 5:2). Auto-detect fasting windows from meal log gaps. Show fasting zones (fat burning, ketosis, autophagy).
- **Why it stands out:** Intermittent fasting is hugely popular. Having it integrated (not a separate app) with full nutrition context is powerful.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 6.5 Allergen & Dietary Preference Alerts
- **What:** Set dietary restrictions (gluten-free, vegan, keto, etc.). Get warnings when logging foods that violate restrictions. AI flags potential allergens in scanned barcodes.
- **Why it stands out:** Safety feature that builds trust and serves dietary-restricted users.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

## 7. Mindfulness & Mental Wellness

### 7.1 Guided Breathing Exercises
- **What:** 1-5 minute breathing exercises (box breathing, 4-7-8, coherent breathing) with haptic guidance and animated visuals. Suggest after high stress detection.
- **Why it stands out:** Actionable stress intervention, not just stress measurement. Closes the loop.
- **Priority:** P0 | **Importance:** I1

### 7.2 Gratitude / Reflection Journal
- **What:** Daily evening prompt: "What went well today?" Quick 1-3 item gratitude entry. Show mood correlation over time.
- **Why it stands out:** Mental wellness + mood tracking together. You already have mood check-in — this deepens it.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

### 7.3 Meditation Timer with Health Tracking
- **What:** Simple meditation timer that records session duration. Cross-reference with HRV and heart rate during/after meditation to show measurable calm.
- **Why it stands out:** Shows meditation's actual physiological impact using data the user already has.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

## 8. Apple Ecosystem Integration

### 8.1 Apple Watch Companion App
- **What:** Quick logging from the wrist — water intake, meal shortcuts, breathing exercises. Show WellPlate Score complication. Real-time stress indicator.
- **Why it stands out:** Wrist-level access dramatically increases logging frequency. Competitors' Watch apps are afterthoughts.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I0

### 8.2 Live Activities & Dynamic Island
- **What:** Show active fasting timer, hydration progress, or daily calorie budget as a Live Activity on the lock screen and Dynamic Island.
- **Why it stands out:** Persistent, glanceable wellness info without opening the app. Modern iOS feature few health apps use well.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

### 8.3 Siri & Shortcuts Integration
- **What:** "Hey Siri, log a glass of water", "Hey Siri, what's my WellPlate Score today?", "Hey Siri, how did I sleep last night?"
- **Why it stands out:** Hands-free logging and queries. Natural extension of voice logging you already have.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

### 8.4 Enhanced Widgets
- **What:** Expand beyond the food widget: WellPlate Score widget, hydration progress widget, step count widget, sleep summary widget, streak widget.
- **Why it stands out:** Multiple home screen widgets keep the app top-of-mind without opening it.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I1

## 9. Data & Privacy

### 9.1 Health Data Export
- **What:** Export all health data as CSV/PDF for sharing with doctors, nutritionists, or personal records. Include charts and AI insights in PDF reports.
- **Why it stands out:** Professional-grade health reports. Useful for doctor visits and health consultations.
- **Priority:** P2 | **Importance:** I2

### 9.2 Privacy-First Marketing
- **What:** All AI processing on-device (Foundation Models). No data leaves the phone. Make this a core brand message.
- **Why it stands out:** In an era of data breaches, privacy is a feature. Competitors send your food data to cloud servers.
- **Priority:** P1 | **Importance:** I0
